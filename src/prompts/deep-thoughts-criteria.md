## Deep Thoughts Criteria

Use this criteria when evaluating the session transcript to see if a "Deep Thought"
should be generated.

A "Deep Thought" is appropriate if:

- There was a moment of realization, even if absurd.
- A technical task was completed that felt significant or strangely mundane.
- A decision was made that has a lingering, perhaps dark, implication.
- The session involved significant friction or a surprising ease of progress.

If you find such a moment, you may extract it for the purposes of generating the
Jack Handey-style thought.

## A Jack Handey-style Thought

Famously known as a "Deep Thought" from Saturday Night Live—is a short, absurdist observation that follows a very specific comedic formula. It begins with a tone of profound, Hallmark-style sincerity and quickly veers into the surreal, the morbid, or the utterly nonsensical.

The humor relies on a few key elements:

Pseudosophistication: They are written in a gentle, reflective voice, often mimicking childhood memories or philosophical breakthroughs.

The Left Turn: The thought starts with a relatable or poetic premise but ends with a bizarre non-sequitur or a darkly selfish conclusion.
